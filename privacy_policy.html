Privacy Policy — Scrapify

Last Updated: July 22, 2026 · Version 5.1 · Android package: com.kabadiwala.app

Hi — this is Scrapify’s privacy policy, written in plain language so you know exactly what happens to your data.

Scrapify is a marketplace and local-services discovery app. You can sell scrap, find kabadiwalas (including email-verified and admin-verified dealers), post legal classified ads, chat with buyers/dealers/workers, share short status updates, request home services, and create a Local Worker profile (skills, rates, work photos) so nearby customers can find you. We are a technology platform — we connect people; we do not buy your scrap or employ local workers ourselves.

This policy covers the mobile app, web app, and related services. It is meant to meet Google Play requirements (Privacy Policy URL, Data Safety, account deletion, permissions) and applicable Indian law, including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 where it applies.

If you do not agree, please do not create an account or use the app.

2. What data we collect — and why

We only collect what we need to run the features you use.

Account (required to sign up)

What: name, email, phone, password or email OTP, role (user or dealer), optional address/city/area, optional profile photo, dealer business details, and email-verification status (isEmailVerified).

Why: create your login, prove you own the email (6-digit code sent to your inbox after signup), show a Verified / Verified Dealer badge on your public profile, and list dealers in the directory.

How: Auth and profile documents are stored in Appwrite Cloud. Signup verification and OTP login use Appwrite email tokens (6-digit codes). Passwords are handled by Appwrite. Until you verify email, the Verified badge is not shown.

Local Workers (optional — same login)

What: skills/categories, worker bio, experience, rate/charges text, availability flag, working hours, up to 10 work-sample photos.

Why: so nearby customers can browse the Local Workers directory and contact you via in-app chat, or Call/WhatsApp when phone visibility allows.

How: stored on your users profile in Appwrite; work photos on Cloudflare R2. Turning off worker profile sets isLocalWorker off and hides you from the directory.

Ratings & reviews

Star ratings (and optional short comments) about dealers/sellers/workers, plus public averages. Typically one review per reviewer→target, stored in Appwrite.

Marketplace ads & scrap rates

Title, description, category, brand/model, price, city/area text, photos, published scrap rates — for legal items only. Listings in Appwrite; photos on R2.

Chat & status

Text messages, optional chat images, status/story images and captions, optional online/busy/offline status. Messages/rooms in Appwrite with participant-level permissions where configured; images on R2 or legacy Storage. Status typically expires ~24 hours.

Order / pickup requests

Name, phone, city, approximate weight, note — usually opens WhatsApp or email. That conversation follows WhatsApp/email policies.

Location (optional)

Approximate GPS and reverse-geocoded city/area only while the app is in use (foreground) — to sort nearby dealers, ads, and Local Workers. No background location. Deny GPS anytime and pick a city manually.

Device & safety

App version, device/OS, theme, crash/diagnostic logs, Expo push token (if allowed). Reports and blocks for spam/fraud/harassment reduction.

We do not ask for bank passwords, UPI PINs, or full card CVV. We do not sell personal data to data brokers or use location for third-party ad networks.

3. Photos & storage (Cloudflare R2 + Appwrite)

  1. The app compresses the image on your device.
  2. After sign-in, a short-lived upload permission is created (Appwrite JWT).
  3. The file is uploaded to Cloudflare R2; a public HTTPS URL is saved with your listing/profile/worker/chat/status record.
  4. Older uploads may still live in Appwrite Storage (legacy).

Public ads, status, and Local Worker profiles are visible to other users; chat images are meant for people in that chat. Account deletion removes associated images where we can reach them (including worker work photos).
